) Overall vibe: “soft premium wellness”
	•	Backgrounds are warm and airy, mostly off-white/cream with slight peach tint. Nothing is pure white.
	•	The UI feels friendly + high-end through:
	•	oversized rounded corners
	•	gentle drop shadows (never harsh)
	•	pastel gradients used as ambient light, not loud color blocks
	•	lots of breathing room (big margins, generous vertical spacing)
	•	There’s a subtle “print/editorial” layer (serif headlines) mixed with modern iOS UI (san-serif supporting text, chips, cards).

⸻

2) Color system: warm neutrals + pastel “glow”

Think of the color palette as two layers:

A) Base neutrals (always present)
	•	Primary canvas: creamy off-white.
	•	Secondary surfaces: slightly darker warm gray/cream.
	•	Dividers/borders: very faint warm gray line (almost imperceptible).

B) Accent pastels (used as highlights)
	•	Pastels are low saturation and often appear as radial or vertical gradients:
	•	peach → pink
	•	lavender → soft purple
	•	pale sky blue → mint
	•	Accents show up as:
	•	circular icon backgrounds
	•	soft gradient “wash” behind content
	•	highlight rings around key controls
	•	No pure black accents. Dark text is a soft charcoal.

⸻

3) Typography: editorial headline + quiet UI text

Headline style
	•	Headings are a high-contrast serif (editorial / magazine feel).
	•	Weight is medium to semi-bold.
	•	Line height is tight but not cramped (e.g., ~1.15–1.25).
	•	Headline text uses title case and spans multiple lines.
	•	Headlines are left aligned on some screens, center aligned on others depending on layout.

Supporting text
	•	Body/captions are a clean sans-serif (iOS system style feel).
	•	Smaller text is light/regular weight and uses higher line height for airiness.
	•	Most supporting text is in muted gray, never competing with the headline.

⸻

4) Layout principles: big margins, centered “hero,” floating modules
	•	Huge corner radii everywhere:
	•	Screen-level cards: very rounded (think “pillowy rectangle”).
	•	Chips/buttons: fully pill-shaped.
	•	Small tiles: rounded rectangles with soft shadows.
	•	Content typically sits inside a tall rounded container (like a card framed within the screen) with:
	•	thin border or subtle shadow
	•	large internal padding
	•	The main UI often has a center hero element (e.g., a phone mock or primary card), with smaller controls floating around it.

⸻

5) Key UI components (and how they look)

5.1 Top brand header
	•	A small logo mark (simple rounded square icon) + brand name.
	•	Positioned near top, with lots of whitespace around it.
	•	Very minimal—no heavy navigation chrome.

5.2 Segmented control / tabs (top of content)
	•	Looks like two pill chips side-by-side.
	•	Each pill contains:
	•	a small colored icon on the left (inside its own tiny rounded square/circle)
	•	a title line
	•	a smaller subtitle line
	•	Selected state is slightly brighter with clearer contrast; unselected is more faded.
	•	The segmented container itself is subtle—almost like two floating pills rather than a big bar.

5.3 “Chips” row (micro-status / categories)
	•	Small pill chips with tiny icons and short labels.
	•	Very soft backgrounds, minimal outlines.
	•	These feel tappable but gentle—no loud borders.

5.4 Floating circular action buttons (icon bubbles)
	•	A signature element: a row of circular buttons that float mid-screen.
	•	Each is:
	•	a perfect circle with a pastel tint background
	•	a centered simple icon
	•	a soft shadow that lifts it off the surface
	•	One central bubble is often emphasized:
	•	slightly larger
	•	may have a gradient fill or stronger glow
	•	can contain icon + a short label/metric underneath

5.5 Primary “card” surfaces
	•	Cards are creamy-white with:
	•	big radius corners
	•	soft shadow (feels like hovering 4–8dp)
	•	Inside cards:
	•	title text in dark gray
	•	microcopy in lighter gray
	•	icons sit in small colored circles or rounded squares

5.6 Stats tiles (3-up row)
	•	Three compact tiles in a row, each:
	•	rounded rectangle
	•	icon at top (in a tinted circle)
	•	label + value underneath
	•	They float over content slightly (layered look).
	•	Visual hierarchy: icon → short label → numeric value.

5.7 “Glass”/frosted overlay card (callout)
	•	There’s a prominent overlay callout card that feels like:
	•	a rounded rectangle
	•	slightly translucent/airy (even if not truly transparent)
	•	with a soft gradient tint at edges
	•	It includes:
	•	headline text on left
	•	tiny illustration/doodle on right (playful line art)
	•	a wide pill button at bottom

5.8 Primary button (pill)
	•	Wide, low-height pill.
	•	Background is near-white or very light warm gray.
	•	Text is centered, medium weight.
	•	Shadow is minimal; it feels “pressable” via motion/haptics rather than heavy contrast.

⸻

6) Iconography & illustration style
	•	Icons are simple, friendly, rounded, typically single-color glyphs.
	•	Each icon usually sits inside a tinted circular background.
	•	Occasional playful illustration appears as thin black line doodles (stars/sparkles/hand gesture) for warmth.

⸻

7) Interaction patterns (how it should feel)

Tap feedback
	•	Micro-scale animation:
	•	buttons slightly compress (scale to ~0.97)
	•	shadow tightens a little
	•	Soft haptic on primary actions (light impact).
	•	No sharp “ink” ripples; it’s iOS-native subtlety.

Navigation transitions
	•	Screen changes should feel:
	•	slowish + floaty
	•	like cards sliding on a cushion of air
	•	Use easing that ramps gently in/out (avoid snappy linear motion).

Cards and overlays
	•	Overlays appear as:
	•	rising from below with slight fade
	•	background dims minimally (not dark; more like a warm gray veil at ~10–20%)
	•	Dismiss by:
	•	swipe down
	•	tap outside (if appropriate)

Segmented control changes
	•	Selection animates with a sliding highlight or subtle background shift.
	•	Text/icon contrast increases on selection.

Floating bubble row
	•	When selecting a bubble:
	•	it enlarges slightly
	•	surrounding bubbles subtly shift outward or dim
	•	the selected one feels “centered” in attention

⸻

8) Spacing, sizing, and radii (practical guidance)

Use these as relative rules (you can adapt exact numbers):
	•	Outer margins: large (think 24–32pt feel).
	•	Card padding: 16–24pt.
	•	Corner radii:
	•	big containers: 28–36
	•	tiles: 18–24
	•	pills: 999 (fully rounded)
	•	icon bubbles: perfect circles (44–56pt typical, hero bubble 60–72pt)
	•	Shadows:
	•	always soft blur, low opacity, slightly warm/neutral
	•	no hard drop shadows or thick borders

⸻

9) Text hierarchy rules (super consistent)
	•	One dominant serif headline per screen.
	•	Everything else is quiet and supportive:
	•	short labels
	•	small descriptions
	•	numbers/metrics in medium emphasis
	•	Keep contrast comfortable: charcoal text, not black.

⸻

10) Accessibility in this style (without breaking aesthetics)

To keep the “soft” look but remain usable:
	•	Ensure tinted icon backgrounds still give enough contrast for glyphs.
	•	Increase contrast on selected chips/buttons via:
	•	slightly darker text
	•	clearer shadow/lift
	•	subtle border
	•	Support Dynamic Type by letting:
	•	serif headlines wrap more
	•	tiles stack into 2 rows when needed
